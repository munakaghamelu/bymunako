Lupita

There's something about seeing african culture on the big screen - its almost magical.

Lupita is a result of the excitement that came after watching Black Panther. I have never seen blackness celebrated on such a large scale. Our culture wasn't being exploited, appropiated or speculated. As well as finding inspiration in art, I also find inspiration in such visuals.

So I want this section to be a source of visual inspiration; I will be updating it regularly with videos that currently inspire or bring joy. If you ever need to get away from your thoughts, or life is trying you, you can come to this page.


Since the start of this summer my interest in the application of AI in art has been growing, this video beautifully illustrates the potential of the intersectionality between tech and art. How This Guy Uses A.I. to Create Art.




The way the brain works is beyond comprehension, this is beyond talent, its a gift. The Artist Who Paints What She Hears.




Jim Carrey: I Needed Color. Simple yet powerful.




The one and only Kehinde Wiley. Kehinde Wiley: John Singer Sargent.




As summer is drawing to an end, I've been lamenting alot about London and the art scene. Although I know its important to try and stay positive and make the best out of these trying times, I have really been missing the little things like going to galleries. This video is a result of that, I found it on a whim. I hope you enjoy it as much as I did. How London Inspires Artists.
