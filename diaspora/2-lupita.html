The social media hype, the constant display of black joy and undying anticipation. Black Panther was one for the community.

Argurably the greatest superhero movie of all time. Don't get me wrong I couldn't stay focused enough to watch the 3 hour end game movie so i'm not expect with regard to the Marvel universe. But when I went to the cinema to watch Black Panther with my sister and mum (who happily brought Jollof Rice and even offed her wig during the movie) I left singing along to SZA's 'All the stars' as soon as the end credits came rolling. So why was black panther so important?

Representation. Just for fun, this i'm going to firstly break down the 'ALl the stars' music video by Kendrick and Sza and then i'll better explain the significance of representation.
