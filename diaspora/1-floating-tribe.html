Floating Tribe

Possibly a direct exploration of my Igbo heritage.

The vast majority of Nigerians I have met have either been Yoruba or Igbo. I remember as a child attending a New Yam festival in Manchester, the highlight of that memory was stacking up on TomToms and playing hide and seek. Being Igbo has always been a subconscious part of my identity.

To break down the naming of this piece, Tribe is a direct reference to how there are so many different tribes not only in Nigeria but within Africa. So many traditions yet to be explored and so much culture yet to be experienced. Culture is truly a beautiful thing.

Floating has both negative and positive connotations. The act of floating could lead to a sense of freedom. Like when you were first taught as a child to float on water; primarily you feel fear but once you begin to float, you are washed over by a sense of calm and accomplishment. Floating can also mean you are drifting away from something, unable to grasp at the thing you are longing for. This is the meaning I have chosen for this piece. Floating tribe, is an ode to forgotten or undiscovered culture. Due to the lack of really knowing your heritage.
Floating Tribe depicts my longing to know more about my Igbo identity.

I haven't been back home consciously... what I mean by this is, at an age where I can fully experience the culture and the country. I do hope to travel back to Nigeria and learn more about my culture in the near future. So this painting is a manifestation of that longing.

The background is an emphasis on the idea of displacement, a tribal woman floating in the clouds. Drifting away from the ground, because her beliefs and practices have not fully taken root. Knowledge not yet embedded in the minds of her descendants. Her knecklace replaced by clouds; an intricate piece of her identity, a result of complacency and ignorance to study one's history.
