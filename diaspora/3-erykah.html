Erykah

Poetry and art, birds of a feather.

Daises.
These daisies will die if chosen by a curious eye,
Like flowers held then left to coil and dry,
Intrigue grown from beauty is always short lived,
Only infatuation is found behind the corners of eyelids.
I was a flower, always seen, adored and picked,
Only to find myself locked within cabinets.
My petals wrinkled, my stems sagged,
I wanted the pedestrians to pass,
But in this field of flowers I've died with every glance,
In murder men always find romance.
I've envied trees, I've envied plants,
There is nothing beautiful about being damned.

A poem by Ariola Adegbite.

I was really inspired by Ari's poems, and how beautifully vivid they were. So I asked her if I could paint one of them and she sent me Daises. I wanted to see how well I could visualise her words. A challenge and an experiment. Can a painting convey the same meaning as a poem?

Here's a link to her instagram, please follow her @pabitez.
She's an amazingly talented individual.
