Diaspora

the dispersion or spread of any people from their original homeland.

I began this series in the summer of 2019. Free from A levels and apprehensive to create. As mentioned in my Artist section, I'm an avid overthinker. So I began reflecting on the past 2 years of my life. Having moved from the lively city of Manchester to rural Cheshire, to say it was simply a cultural shock would be the understatement of the century. Nevertheless, I was determined to navigate this new space and still make something out of myself. So there I was, during a night shift, questioning my identity.

I felt as though I had lost a part of myself, my culture and possibly my "blackness". Are we simply products of our environment? I came to the premature conclusion that moving out of Manchester was synonymous with losing my identity.

I was in a state of constant conflicting thoughts and emotions. I knew I missed my city, the culture and the friends I grew up with. But what I didn't know was how much I had changed in 2 years. Don't get me wrong, I made new friends and still played basketball (a sport that shaped most of my high school experience).

So what changed? I definelty felt as though I was more introverted and yes - more of an overthinker. I had never been in a space where I was the only one that looked like me.

So it was after my first painting Floating Tribe, I decided to make this into a Series. Diaspora is an ode to rediscovering myself. Discovering who I had become and the things in society that influence who I am. I guess 'Diaspora' to me is a Nigerian-Brit trying to navigate a space outside her comfort zone. I wanted to illustrate my thoughts.

Diaspora allowed me to understand that growth isn't finite, being black is complicated and self love is really just trying not to be so hard on yourself.
